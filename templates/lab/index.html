Theme

Developmental Biology Program · Sloan Kettering Institute, MSKCC

hPSCs as a model to probe
the logic of human cell fate

We use human pluripotent stem cells as a genetic model to interrogate transcriptional and epigenetic mechanisms underlying cell-fate decisions in development and disease. Large-scale CRISPR screening, functional epigenomics, and computational approaches converge on a central question: how do gene regulatory programs specify — and maintain — cell identity during human development?

Research

What we work on

Our lab combines genome-scale CRISPR screening, functional epigenomics, and computational genomics in hPSCs to dissect the regulatory logic of cell-fate decisions in development and disease.

◈

Pancreatic Development & β Cell Biology

We study the transcriptional and epigenetic programs that drive pancreatic lineage commitment and β cell maturation, using hPSC differentiation as a tractable human model.

⬡

Genome-Scale CRISPR Screening

We develop and apply large-scale CRISPRi and knockout screens to systematically identify genes and regulatory elements controlling cell identity across developmental transitions.

◎

Epigenetic Regulation & DNA Methylation

We investigate how noncoding regulatory elements — particularly DNA methylation valleys and developmental enhancers — are established and maintained to control gene expression.

◻

Therapeutic Strategies for Diabetes

Mechanistic insights from our developmental studies directly inform cell-based and gene-therapy approaches for the prevention and reversal of type 1 and type 2 diabetes.
